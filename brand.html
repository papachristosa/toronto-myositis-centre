The mark

The M-Line Seal.

A serif M for Myositis inside a disc. A single maple line, the M-line at the centre of every striated muscle unit, runs through the letter and past the circle on both sides. It is a mark that does not stay inside its own lines: rare disease, met with routine excellence.

Primary
Navy disc on warm cream
Reverse
Cream disc on navy
Single colour
One ink, for emboss or fax
Colour

The palette.

Navy and cream carry the system. Maple is the one accent, used sparingly for the M-line, alerts, and calls to action. Teal marks movement and rehabilitation.

Usage

A few rules.

Do

  • Keep clear space around the seal equal to the height of the letter M.
  • Use the primary mark on cream or white, the reverse on navy.
  • Let the maple M-line run past the circle. That break is the point.
  • Reserve maple for the mark, alerts, and one call to action per view.

Avoid

  • Recolouring the seal or filling the disc with any colour but navy or cream.
  • Placing the primary (navy) mark on a dark or busy background.
  • Stretching, rotating, or adding effects to the mark.
  • Using maple for large fields or body text.
